Metering Phases

There are 5 metering phases: not started, early metering, metering, flushing and stopped. A ramp meter begins a metering period in the not started phase. When transition conditions are met, the meter moves to the next phase. Also, when in the stopped phase, the meter may transition back to the metering phase.

Shut-Off

Before testing shut-off logic for a meter, the 15 minute segment density must be trending downward for each interval in the last 5 minutes, or the segment density must be below 24.05 vehicles / lane-mile.

  • If the average segment density was above the desired density (29.6) for any interval in the last 5 minutes, continue metering.
  • If passage rate (60 second smoothed) was lower than metering rate for all intervals in the last 5 minutes, shut off meter.
  • If segment has no bottleneck for at least 5 minutes, shut off meter.
  • Otherwise, continue metering.

Definitions

Density
Vehicles per lane-mile
Station Density
Average of all valid lanes in a station for one sample period
Interval Density
Average of station samples during one 60 second interval
Meter Segment
A contiguous series of stations associated with a ramp meter. It begins at the station just upstream of the meter. The downstream end is the first bottleneck within 3 miles (or the last station within 3 miles if no bottleneck exists).
Segment Density
Each consecutive pair of stations is divided into 3 equal links. The first and last links are assigned density from the station adjacent to them. The middle link is assigned a density which is an average of the two stations. The segment density is an average of all links in the segment, weighted by length.
